# SRT and the neuroscience of consciousness (bridge)

> A bridge / lab note: it applies SRT's primitives to consciousness mechanisms; it does **not** define `d-value`, `Ψf`, consciousness, pathology, diagnosis, or treatment. Formulas marked as gates or proxies are candidate lab models, not established neural theorems.
>
> **Necessary, not sufficient.** Local neural ignition, thin `L1`, high `d-value`, re-entrant stabilization, or report access are not sufficient for subjecthood. Micro-selection, access, and salience do **not** automatically entail consciousness-as-subjecthood.

## 1. The core question

> Consciousness is not just "information being broadcast." So how does the nervous system *confirm* a candidate content into a reportable, maintainable reality?

SRT's compressed answer: consciousness = selection confirmed and stabilized; ignition = an `L0 → L1` phase transition; binding = synchronous selection into one manifest object; sleep = structural maintenance of future choice capacity. More precisely:

> **Consciousness is not mere information processing; it is concern-weighted, stable `L1` anchoring.**

Conscious content is not any local activation or stimulus complexity itself, but a candidate state that becomes `L1` when it is sufficiently activated, gain-modulated, gated through, recursively stabilized, and coupled to report, action, self-model, or future deposition. `d-value` does not by itself produce consciousness, but it modulates the *thickness*, priority, persistence, and deposition potential of conscious content.

## 2. Ignition: not broadcast, but candidate gating

A candidate ignites when activation passes threshold and the integration–concern product passes a critical value:

$$\mathcal{A}(\sigma) \ge \tau_{ignite} \;\land\; \Phi_{proxy}\cdot d_{proxy} > C_{critical}$$

This is SRT's candidate rewrite of global-workspace ignition; it needs an independent measurement window and cannot be inferred from report rate alone. The multiplicative form is a *structural preference*, not a settled theorem — additive and probabilistic gates remain live alternatives:

| Gate reading | Status | Favored when |
|---|---|---|
| Multiplicative `Φ·d` | structural preference | low value in either factor blocks ignition; interaction terms improve prediction |
| Additive `w_Φ·Φ + w_d·d` | operational fallback | one factor can compensate for the other |
| Probabilistic `P(ignite)` | lab-facing model | access is graded, noisy, or report-confidence mediated |

**Re-entrant selection** then stabilizes the candidate into reportable reality — not replay, but stabilization:

$$\mathcal{R}_{re} : L_1^{candidate} \rightarrow L_1^{stabilized}$$

**Thin vs. thick `L1`** avoids a crude conscious/unconscious binary:

| State | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Thin `L1` | weak, unstable, hard to report, weak action/self coupling | vague hunches, tip-of-the-tongue, peripheral flashes, dream residue |
| Thick `L1` | stable, reportable/actionable, strongly coupled to body/self/value, likely to deposit as `L2` | pain, clear danger, major decisions, shame, trauma, insight |

$$\text{thickness}(x) \sim S(x)\cdot G(x)\cdot A(x)\cdot M(x)\cdot d(x)$$

(stabilization · global access · action coupling · self-model coupling · concern weight). This is a bridge/lab indicator structure, not a physical law.

## 3. Binding: not concatenation, but topological synchrony

$$\mathcal{B} : \{\sigma_i\} \rightarrow \sigma_{bound}, \qquad \Delta\phi_i \rightarrow 0$$

Binding is not gluing ready-made features together; it is letting scattered features be *co-selected* into one time–phase frame. If phases cannot converge, `σ_bound` fails to exist — yielding fragmented experience, dissociation, or a destabilized sense of a unified object.

## 4. Consciousness is not infinite bandwidth

$$B_{consc} < B_{max}, \qquad \sum_k \mathcal{A}_k \le B_{consc}$$

Consciousness is a bandwidth-competition system, not an infinite container. High-risk-gradient content preempts bandwidth: pain has priority not because it is "louder" but because it carries a higher survival-risk gradient — in `d-value` terms, high consequence-weight for bodily maintenance, action interruption, self-involvement, and future behavior.

## 5. Time: continuity from coupled discrete selections

$$\tau_{select} = \tau_{sensory} + \tau_{integrative} + \tau_{gate}$$

Consciousness has latency; it is not real-time presentation but the delayed result of integration and gating. Continuous time-experience is itself a product of fast-wave content coupled to a slow-wave frame, not a direct given.

## 6. Sleep: not shutdown, but structural maintenance

$$\mathcal{S}_{sleep} : \hat{G}_\theta \rightarrow \hat{G}_\theta^{repair}$$

$$\Delta L_2 \propto \int_{sleep} \nabla_\theta F \, dt$$

Sleep is not pausing consciousness but keeping structure available for future consciousness. Dreams and replay are not pure noise but re-weighting and consolidation of future selection rules — internal candidates forming a temporary `L1` under weak external constraint, often with high emotional `d-value` but weak coupling to action and stable `L2`, hence easily collapsing on waking.

## 7. Traveling waves: who enters reality first is not only about strength

$$\mathcal{W}(x,t) = A(x,t)\, e^{i\phi(x,t)}$$

Traveling waves are not a background rhythm but a consciousness *routing field*. A candidate probabilistic coupling:

$$P_{ignite} = \sigma\!\left(\alpha\, C_{wave} + \beta\,(\Phi\cdot d) + \gamma\, D_{align} - \delta\right)$$

Ignition depends not only on total activation but on coherence and whether direction matches the task. If direction is mismatched, reportability drops — not because there was "no activation" but because the routing was wrong.

## 8. Agency and operator short-circuit

$$A_{sub}(t) = \frac{1}{1 + \lambda\,\lvert \sigma_{L_1}(t) - \hat{G}_\theta^{predict}(L_2)\rvert}$$

The feeling of "I am the author" comes from the match between current manifestation and the internal predictive structure. Habit and addiction can be rewritten as `L2` directly hijacking the behavior path, bypassing the explicit selection that would otherwise be required — which is why one can "know it's wrong" yet still act, and why behavior can precede the conscious narrative. (In neural terms this bridges only to habituation, gating rigidity, and re-entry loss — not to claims about legitimacy or institutions.)

## 9. Boundary cases and psychopathology

| Case | SRT reading |
|---|---|
| Blindsight | info enters local processing and action channels, but lacks thick `L1` anchoring |
| Bistable perception | candidates compete in accessible `L0`, alternately winning `L1` stabilization |
| Pain | a high-`d-value` candidate forcibly restructures current `L1` |
| Dreaming | internal candidates form a temporary `L1` under weak external constraint |
| Meditation | training may reduce automatic hardening of candidates, shifting `d-value` / `Ψf` allocation |
| Anesthesia | candidate activation, recursive stabilization, or global gating is broken; no thick `L1` forms |

Psychopathology is read not as "too much / too little consciousness" but as distortions in anchoring thickness, concern-weight, closing-friction, and `L2` deposition: social anxiety (others'-evaluation candidates get excessive `d-value`); shame trauma (social high-`d-value` `L1` over-deposits into self-related `L2`); OCD (risk candidates enter `L1` and cannot be closed cheaply); addiction (low-friction `L2` bypasses explicit re-selection); delusional salience (low-evidence candidates over-granted reality weight).

## 10. Relation to GNWT, IIT, and predictive processing

| Framework | What it explains | SRT-specific addition |
|---|---|---|
| GNWT / Global workspace | how info becomes globally accessible, reportable, usable | the candidate competition *before* access, the `Ψf` *at* access, the `L2` hardening *after* |
| IIT | integration / intrinsic causal structure and experience | what *has consequence* for the system, and how that consequence becomes stable `L1` |
| Predictive processing / FEP | how models explain input and self-maintain | how a candidate gains reality status, why it has `d-value`, how it deposits into future terrain |

> Global workspace explains access; SRT explains anchoring.
> IIT emphasizes integration; SRT emphasizes concern-weighted stabilization.
> Predictive processing explains model updating; SRT explains lived-reality anchoring.

## 11. Conclusion (seven sentences)

1. Consciousness is not broadcast itself, but the selection phase-transition that confirms and stabilizes a candidate into reality.
2. It is not information "lighting up," but a candidate possibility anchored into `L1` through body, action, self, and concern.
3. Binding is not feature concatenation, but scattered trajectories synchronously co-selected into one object.
4. Conscious bandwidth is limited; high-`d-value` content preempts manifestation resources.
5. Sleep is not shutdown, but necessary maintenance of `L2` and future choice capacity.
6. Reportability and the sense of agency depend not only on activation strength but on routing direction, predictive match, and whether an operator short-circuit occurred.
7. Pathological conscious states are imbalances of anchoring thickness, closing-friction, and historical hardening.

## Hardest objections (what would force a retraction)

1. **Conscious access may be fully explained by global-workspace access.** SRT's answer: it does not deny access; it *adds* pre-access selection, `d-value`, `Ψf`, and post-access hardening as incremental variables. — If that increment shows nothing, retract "anchoring beyond access" and fall back to a GNWT translation layer.
2. **`d-value` may collapse into arousal, salience, or report confidence.** Answer: `d-value` must predict memory, bodily reaction, action change, and `L2` deposition *beyond* salience/confidence. — Else retract concern-weighted thickness as an independent claim.
3. **Thin/thick `L1` may be a report-scale artifact.** Answer: it must be tested by non-report markers — action coupling, memory persistence, bodily reaction, future-selection change. — Else keep graded-access language but drop the thickness ontology.
4. **High-`d-value` *unconscious* processes challenge the d–consciousness coupling.** Answer: `d-value` is not sufficient for consciousness; it modulates priority and thickness only *after* gating and stabilization. — If it has no measurable effect on stabilization or deposition, weaken it to a peripheral motivational variable.
5. **Subjecthood may require more than conscious-content anchoring.** Answer: this note treats mechanisms and content anchoring; subjecthood is routed through separate threshold conditions. — Retract any claim that local access, salience, high `d-value`, or reportability is sufficient for subjecthood.
