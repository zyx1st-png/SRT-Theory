# SRT critical-objections FAQ

> This FAQ addresses the most common objections to SRT, sorting each into *already argued / testable / still open*. Its purpose is not to declare SRT correct, but to make the theory clear enough to be argued with. (This is an English version of the Chinese FAQ in this repo.)

---

## Q1. Is SRT falsifiable, or just a nice story?

**Short answer:** falsifiable, but distinguish levels.

- **Directly testable:** behavioral, neural, physiological, and social-network predictions.
- **Moderately testable:** cross-scale mappings and parameter stability (need more complex equipment and protocols).
- **Highly abstract:** the unifying metaphysical claims (evidence comes mainly from explanatory power + consistency + counterexample pressure).

**Refutation principle:** if core predictions keep failing across scenarios, samples, and pre-registered conditions, the corresponding SRT sub-claims must be downgraded or revised.

---

## Q2. Is SRT panpsychism (everything has a mind)?

**Short answer:** no.

SRT allows `d-value` to operate isomorphically across scales, but distinguishes clearly: mathematical isomorphism ≠ identity of phenomenal experience; care and consciousness emerge only when embodiment / risk and similar conditions are met. An electron may have structural parameters; that does not mean an electron "cares."

---

## Q3. You've defined `d-value` in several versions — is that self-contradictory?

**Short answer:** there is one main definition; the others are context-specific expressions.

- **Canonical:** $d \equiv \lVert \partial\mathcal{U}/\partial\mathcal{S} \rVert$
- Cognitive form (e.g. $\alpha A + \beta\log V + \gamma\tau$) is an approximate projection.
- Cross-scale forms ($d_{quantum}, d_{bio}, d_{cosmic}$) are mapping instances.

Criterion: any "d definition" that does not link back to the canonical one counts only as a local operationalization.

---

## Q4. What is SRT's relationship to the Free Energy Principle (FEP)?

**Short answer:** SRT contains and extends the FEP context.

- FEP is strong on "how a system stabilizes its internal model."
- SRT goes further into "what constitutes anchored reality and selection structure in the first place."

Read it as: in SRT, FEP is one key dynamical component, not the whole ontological framework. (For the careful, formula-level version, see [`SRT_FEP_interface.md`](SRT_FEP_interface.md).)

---

## Q5. Is SRT smuggling in religion or mysticism?

**Short answer:** allowing dialogue is not smuggling.

SRT can be compared with Buddhist thought, phenomenology, and systems theory, but its validity still rests on (1) formal consistency, (2) falsifiable predictions, (3) compatibility with data. If a passage is only analogy with no testable content, it should be explicitly marked `[Speculative]`.

---

## Q6. Won't SRT become an "explains everything" theory?

**Short answer:** this is one of the biggest risks, and must be actively prevented.

Anti-runaway rules: every claim must state its refutation conditions; specify scope and failure scenarios; refuse post-hoc patch explanations (HARKing). If a proposition can never possibly fail, it is not a scientific proposition.

---

## Q7. Why doesn't high AI performance equal high consciousness?

**Short answer:** performance is function-approximation capacity; consciousness in SRT requires risk-coupling and an embodied boundary.

Under current pure-software architectures, an "error" usually does not produce irreversible survival cost, so the canonical concern-gradient `d` may approach 0. This is a theoretical claim, not a moral put-down.

---

## Q8. How do you avoid forcing ethical conclusions into physical equations?

**Short answer:** distinguish three output layers.

1. **Descriptive:** how the system runs (equations, statistics).
2. **Predictive:** if you intervene on X, how Y changes.
3. **Normative:** what we ought to do (value choices).

SRT can supply constraint information to the normative layer, but cannot replace ethical decision itself.

---

## Q9. If an experiment returns a negative result, what should SRT do?

**Standard procedure:** (1) first check whether the operationalization is distorted (is the proxy reasonable?); (2) after replicating the failure, publish the negative result; (3) downgrade the corresponding claim to a local / conditional hypothesis; (4) update the documents and version record. The goal is a *revisable theory*, not an *untouchable creed*.

---

## Q10. How can an outside reader quickly judge whether SRT is worth reading further?

Look at three things: (1) does it give a clear canonical definition (rather than a pile of concepts)? (2) does it have explicit falsifiable predictions? (3) will negative results be formally written back into the theory? Only when all three hold is it worth investing more time.

---

## Q11. Doesn't SRT demote the scientific method to an "illusion"?

**Short answer:** no. SRT criticizes *overreach*, not the method itself.

The scientific method is the strongest tool within its domain of applicability; the point of contention is upgrading "the method works" directly into "the ontology is complete." Operational standard: any claim that "a methodological conclusion = an ultimate ontological conclusion" needs an extra cross-layer proof; otherwise it counts only as a within-framework conclusion.

---

## Q12. If the neural correlates (NCC) are highly stable, why still not identical to experience?

**Short answer:** stable correlation ≠ identity.

NCC is the observable trace left when experience occurs; experience is the first-person manifestation of the selection act, not replaceable by a single layer. "Can be induced" does not entail "has been reduced"; to claim identity, one must explain why first-person differences must vanish under homomorphic neural trajectories.

---

## Q13. Is SRT's "surrender / passive alignment" anti-action?

**Short answer:** no. It is a re-parameterization of the control variable, not a halt.

Passive alignment is defined as: local grasping cost goes down, global coordinative payment goes up; the result should be "clarity ↑, consistency ↑," not "agency → zero." Failure criterion: if, after claiming to enter passive alignment, a system shows long-term goal collapse and execution decay, then that state is not the passive-alignment phase transition SRT defines.
