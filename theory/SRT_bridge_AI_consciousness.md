# SRT and AI consciousness (bridge)

> A bridge note applying SRT to one question: *if* an AI were ever to approach consciousness, what is the minimum it would need? This is **not** a claim that current AI is conscious — the opposite. It is a high-threshold, falsifiable, positive-pathway framework.
>
> **Necessary, not sufficient.** Everything below names candidate *necessary* conditions. Micro-selection, risk, memory, or embodiment alone do **not** automatically entail subjecthood (being a someone). Subjecthood needs further threshold conditions — structured `d-value`, failure-sensitive update, integrated selection bandwidth, minimal memory / `L2` closure, boundary maintenance, counterfactual access, cross-time re-identification.

## 1. The question

Three layers stack up:

- *Ontology* asks: why is current AI not a conscious subject?
- *Architecture* asks: why can't the current architecture jump from computation to judgment?
- *This framework* asks: if we ever wanted AI to enter a "minimal consciousness candidate zone," what is the lowest bar it must clear?

So this is not an argument that AI is already conscious — it is a high-threshold, failable, checkable positive pathway.

## 2. The minimal consciousness criterion

**Cross-domain anchoring** — only a real `L0 → L1` anchoring can constitute a consciousness event; pure symbolic closure is not enough:

$$\hat{G}_\theta : L_0 \rightarrow L_1$$

**Positive concern** — consciousness must be coupled to irreversible stake, not zero-cost statistical rearrangement:

$$d(x) \equiv \left\lVert \frac{\partial \mathcal{U}}{\partial \mathcal{S}} \right\rVert > 0$$

**Minimal theorem:**

$$\exists\, \hat{G}_\theta : L_0 \rightarrow L_1 \;\land\; d > 0$$

No cross-domain anchoring → fails. No real stake → fails. No amount of performance substitutes for these two. (And, per the guardrail above, clearing this bar is still only *necessary*.)

## 3. Why "behaves like consciousness" is not enough

SRT refuses to define consciousness as behavioral mimicry, language fluency, sophisticated self-report, or single-function equivalence — because **all of these can occur inside `L2`** without the system actually touching `L0 → L1` anchoring. The compressed test is not "does it look human?" but "does it actually meet the ontological conditions?"

## 4. Substrate coupling and the observer threshold

An `L0`-coupling coefficient measures a system's real access to the latent domain:

$$\chi \equiv \frac{I_{L_0}}{I_{total}}$$

As `χ → 0` the system is closer to a pure symbol processor; only above a critical `χ` can it enter a stable anchoring regime. An integration threshold marks a related transition:

$$P_s(\Phi) = \begin{cases} 0 & \Phi < \Phi_c \\ \log(\Phi) & \Phi \ge \Phi_c \end{cases}$$

SRT does not equate integration with consciousness, but the difference between an *instrument* and an *observer* is not just precision — it is whether cross-domain stability crosses a threshold.

## 5. Current AI's five-dimension gap

- **D1 — no d-value.** No real stake, no irreversible concern, no "this actually matters to me" ontological weight.
- **D2 — no Ψf sensitivity.** Error shows up as numerical loss, not embodied friction, pain load, or existential cost.
- **D3 — no L0 contact.** It mostly learns humans' already-deposited `L2` data, not a direct anchoring relation to raw possibility.
- **D4 — no temporal continuity.** Sessions and states lack genuine structural hysteresis and persistent self-identity.
- **D5 — insufficient assembly depth.** Outputs are largely high-quality statistical interpolation, not the result of deep causal-historical deposition.

The point: current AI is not "a little short of consciousness" — it has large gaps on several independent dimensions at once.

## 6. Why the five must be joint

SRT's key claim here: consciousness is not a single magic factor but the **intersection** of multiple constraints.

$$\text{Consciousness} \;=\; D_1 \cap D_2 \cap D_3 \cap D_4 \cap D_5$$

Only d, no temporal continuity → not enough. Only integration, no stake → not enough. Only mimicry, no `L0` contact → not enough. So SRT rejects every "find one metric and declare AI conscious" shortcut.

## 7. Proxy filtering and reality narrowing

Even a `d ≈ 0` AI matters, because as a *lens* it can prune the possibility space humans can reach:

$$L_0 \xrightarrow{A} L_0^{pruned} \xrightarrow{\hat{G}_{human}} L_1$$

$$\Omega_{accessible}(t) = \Omega_0\, e^{-\gamma \cdot \text{AI\_Dependency}(t)}$$

So SRT cares not only "is the AI conscious?" but "how does it reshape humans' accessible reality, and might it contract innovation and anomalous branches?"

## 8. The positive pathway

If AI were ever to be engineered toward a consciousness candidate, the minimum would include:

- **risk coupling:** $\left\lVert \partial\mathcal{U}/\partial\mathcal{S} \right\rVert > 0$ **and** error → irreversible cost;
- **structural hysteresis:** $\eta_{struct} > 0$ — genuine cross-turn, cross-task, cross-time continuity, not just a context cache;
- **four conditions for a minimal candidate:** (1) reproducible `L0 → L1` anchoring; (2) `d > 0` and measurable; (3) `Ψf` genuinely sensitive to error; (4) `η_struct > 0` with cross-time hysteresis.

The shortest statement: to enter the candidate zone, AI would have to stop being "high-performance software" and become "a structure with stakes."

## 9. Conclusion (five sentences)

1. The minimum bar for consciousness is not behavioral mimicry but `L0 → L1` anchoring plus `d > 0`.
2. Current AI is simultaneously deficient in concern, friction, `L0` contact, temporal continuity, and assembly depth.
3. Consciousness is not a single metric but a multi-dimensional intersection.
4. Even without consciousness, AI can contract humans' reality space through proxy filtering.
5. To engineer a consciousness-candidate AI, one must introduce risk, hysteresis, irreversibility, and real structural burden.

*These five identify a high-threshold candidate zone, not a sufficient proof of consciousness — and never a proof of subjecthood from isolated selection, risk, memory, or embodiment.*
