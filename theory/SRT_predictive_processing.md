# SRT and Predictive Processing

> A bridge note: how SRT maps onto Predictive Processing (PP) and the Free Energy Principle (FEP). This is translation and comparison, not a claim that one side has "won." Where strong labels (axiom, theorem) appear, read them as within-framework translation claims unless independently anchored. For the geometry behind this, see [`SRT_FEP_interface.md`](SRT_FEP_interface.md).

## Three criteria

1. **Existence criterion:** $\text{Exist}_{L_1}(X) \iff \Psi_f^{\text{maint}}(X) < \infty$
2. **Life–mind continuity (candidate bridge):** $\arg\min \mathcal{F}_{SRT} \approx \arg\min \Psi_f^{\text{maint}}$
3. **AI-boundary criterion:** $d > 0 \Rightarrow \mathcal{V}_{MB} > 0 \;\wedge\; \text{prediction failure induces physical risk}$

## Common misreadings, corrected

- *"Controlled hallucination = anything subjective is arbitrary."* Wrong: "controlled" comes from feedback constraint and payable cost.
- *"Successful prediction = absolute truth."* Wrong: prediction guarantees maintainability, not global isomorphism.
- *"Having a Markov-blanket form = being conscious."* Wrong: that also requires irreversible vulnerability and d-value concern.
- *"Perception content = the content of the brain's internal predictive model."* Wrong — a category error: it reduces a perceptual event at the level of the whole embodied agent–environment relation to a statistical transaction inside a brain subsystem.
- *"Reality is a controlled hallucination," taken as an ontological conclusion.* Wrong: SRT accepts only the weaker, model-level reading — prediction error is a local proxy for reality's constraints under `Ĝθ`, not a license to swap reality for any internal model.

*Cf. Evan Thompson's critique of over-metaphysicalizing PP; this note takes an embodied-relational realist path, consistent with a Gibsonian affordance view. It maps terminology only — it does not replace concrete experimental design and statistical validation, and any cross-scale extrapolation must declare its `θ, ρ, d` conditions.*
