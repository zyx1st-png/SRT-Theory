# Testability and the experimental program

> SRT is meant to be falsifiable, not just suggestive. This is a prioritized sample of its experimental program: concrete, pre-registrable hypotheses, scored and sequenced, each with minimal observable metrics and explicit falsification discipline. (These are selected high-priority items from a larger hypothesis set.)

Each hypothesis is scored out of 5 on four dimensions — data availability, equipment burden (lower burden scores higher), replicability, and theory leverage (how much it tests the core) — and combined as:

> composite = 0.30 · data + 0.20 · equipment + 0.30 · replicability + 0.20 · leverage

## Minimal observable metrics

- **H22** — public-goods-game contribution rate; stability across repeated rounds; in-group defection rate.
- **H18** — time to consensus; misunderstanding rate; task-completion rate.
- **H7** — reaction time and accuracy on attention-selection tasks; parameter fit.
- **H10** — stress-recovery slope; subjective friction rating; task-switching cost.
- **H19** — post-change retention; conflict-event frequency; coordination efficiency.
- **H9** — PCI (perturbational complexity index), if available, plus a behavioral-complexity composite.
- **H11** — recovery-trajectory slope; relapse rate; intervention response time.

## Discipline

- Behavioral studies are pre-registered, to avoid HARKing (hypothesizing after results are known).
- Every proposed study carries explicit falsification conditions — no one-directional confirmation narratives.
- Neural and clinical items (H9, H11) require ethics approval and cross-institution collaboration.

The intent is a *revisable* theory: if a core prediction keeps failing under pre-registered conditions, the corresponding claim is downgraded or revised, and the negative result is published.
