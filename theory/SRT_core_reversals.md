# Core Reversals

These reversals state SRT's founding force in compact form. They are source-intuition pointers, not replacements for the formal core. Each pairs the reversal with the misreading it most often invites, and the boundary that blocks that misreading.

## 1. Existence is not background

**One sentence.** Existence is not a pre-given background; it is the stabilized trace of selection under constraint, cost, concern, and history.

**Easy misreading.** "SRT says nothing exists until a mind thinks about it."

**Boundary.** SRT is not mind-first idealism. Selection is broader than mental choice, and stable reality is tested by resistance, cost, and convergence.

## 2. The subject is not the premise of selection

**One sentence.** The subject is not the original chooser behind selection; it is a stabilized product of selection processes that have become self-referential enough to hold a position.

**Easy misreading.** "SRT denies subjects, agency, or first-person reality."

**Boundary.** SRT does not erase subjecthood. It relocates subjecthood from primitive premise to structured emergence.

## 3. Attention is not selection itself

**One sentence.** Attention is not selection itself; it is selection becoming visible at the cognitive layer.

**Easy misreading.** "SRT reduces reality to attention."

**Boundary.** Attention is a local cognitive manifestation. SRT's selection structure also includes embodiment, anchoring, cost, history, social convergence, and physical constraint.

## 4. Value is not preference

**One sentence.** Value is not mere preference; it is consequence return where what happens cannot be fully substituted without loss.

**Easy misreading.** "SRT says value is just whatever a system wants."

**Boundary.** Preference can be cheap, reversible, or simulated. SRT value requires stake, consequence return, and non-substitutability.

## 5. Consciousness is not everywhere

**One sentence.** Consciousness is not a property of everything; it is a threshold window where selection enters concern, burden, consequence return, and self-modulation.

**Easy misreading.** "SRT is panpsychism."

**Boundary.** SRT treats selection as broad and consciousness as thresholded. A system can process, optimize, report, or imitate without crossing the relevant threshold.
