# Open problems

> SRT keeps an explicit ledger of what it has *not* yet closed. This is not a weakness display — it is a discipline: an unresolved point must not be cited as if it were settled. Each item lists what is in hand, what is missing, and what would count as progress.

## 1. The origin of selectability

SRT says selection produces existence and needs no pre-existing chooser. What it does **not** yet derive is how the very first selecting capacity arises from a wholly non-selective ground. Bridge accounts (biology, AI, agency) describe selectors that *already exist*; they do not solve the origin. Progress would be a real derivation — or an honestly-marked primitive — that does not quietly smuggle a chooser back in.

## 2. Value (d-value) vs. mere capacity

The canonical definition separates stake-coupled concern from raw discriminative capacity:

$$d_{canonical} \;\le\; D_{eff}.$$

What's missing are sharp necessary-and-sufficient conditions for when a capacity direction is *genuinely* stake-coupled rather than merely "able to tell things apart." Progress would be a tested "stake gate," with explicit failure cases (fake stake, mis-bound stake, absorbed or non-returning consequence). Until then, "capacity" must not be reported as "concern."

## 3. The status of friction (Ψf)

Ψf — the cost of making and holding a reality — has three readings: impedance, paid cost, and geometric path-length. They are unified in intent, but their formal borders are not fully separated. The risk is sliding from "one object, three readings" into "one formula explains all dynamics." Progress would be a typology that fixes where each reading is valid and where it is misuse (e.g. a Fisher length is a lower bound, not necessarily the actual paid cost).

## 4. Formalizing directional readability (T_dir)

T_dir — how well a system reads the direction of its own selection — has a clear role but a thinner formal apparatus than d or Ψf. Open questions: is it a scalar, a relation, or an accessibility function? Does "readability" require conscious access and reportability, or only an internal self-model update? Progress would be the *weakest* formal object that still carries the work, without collapsing T_dir into confidence, valence, or reward alignment.

## 5. Healthy vs. lethal stabilization (L2)

SRT distinguishes structure that *lowers* friction so real choice remains, from structure that *replaces* live choice while hiding the debt. The hard case is high-functioning structure that improves performance and lowers felt friction yet quietly removes contact with live selection — from the outside, "support" and "replacement" can look identical. Progress would be a **structural test**, not a moral preference: Does the structure preserve re-entry into real choice moments? Do consequences return to the system rather than being absorbed elsewhere? Does friction reduction increase future choice capacity, or remove the need to choose at all? Does T_dir survive?

## 6. Minimal surrogate stake in AI

Current AI can have large discriminative capacity without that becoming real stake; performance, persistence, and self-report are not stake, and prediction error usually does not bind the system itself. The open question is whether a non-biological system could acquire a *minimal* but genuinely binding stake — an irreversible consequence that returns into its own future selection capacity. Progress would be a defined sufficiency window (irreversible consequence; return path into the system's own future choice; non-transferable burden; a payability constraint; observable degradation when exceeded). Even meeting it would not, by itself, establish consciousness.

## 7. Stable perspective: entry vs. maintenance

What is the minimal threshold for being perspective-bearing, and how does *becoming* one differ from *staying* one? If these are not kept apart, "stable perspective" risks becoming both the entry condition and the explanation of its own emergence. Progress would be two clearly separated layers — the definitional entry conditions, and the mechanisms by which a process achieves and retains that status.

## 8. "Floor replacement" without "explains everything"

SRT frames itself as replacing the inherited floor (existence-first) with a selection-first one. But a framing thesis only earns its keep if it makes *discriminating* predictions. The failure conditions it holds itself to:

- if Ψf produces no transition-cost signature distinguishable from ordinary loss, prediction error, energy expenditure, or task difficulty, its operational role weakens;
- if d-value does not predict concern-weighted non-substitutability better than reward, preference, salience, or pain, its distinct role weakens;
- if L2-hardening cannot be distinguished from ordinary memory, habit, or convention, its bridge role weakens;
- if the selection → manifestation → hardening loop yields no domain-specific prediction beyond FEP, predictive processing, reinforcement learning, IIT/GNW, or social constructionism, the cross-scale claim collapses into analogy.

Progress would be **at least one domain where SRT predicts a pattern its rivals do not.** That is the bar it has set for itself.
